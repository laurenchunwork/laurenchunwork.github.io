Overview

Samsung Research America, is Samsung Electronics’ American lab where they develop a range of technologies for their solutions.

My team was asked to create potential use cases for solutions that demonstrates the breath of capacities of the technology helping identified users.

My Role

I was the ux designer and researcher on a team of 2 designers, 1 engineer, 3 tech artists, 1 supervisor, and 3 leads.

I was in charge of leading communicating with our supervisor for advocating our designs.

Focus

  • Secondary Research
  • Developing use cases
  • User flows
  • Creating assets and prefabs for figma and unity prototypes

Design Process

Prompt

Each prompt was different based on the user, technology, and goals.

Objective

  • Research trends and determine highest value use cases
  • Develop proof of concept prototype solutions with technology to showcase to stakeholders and share for further development of product.

Understanding Problems

Our director provides

  • a type of user to target
  • a technology and solution we want to showcase

From our research findings, we ideated how might we tackle trends of specific demographics and identify potential opportunities.

Developing Use Cases

From our research we ideation to create use cases.

A scenario and user journey are created of how the user would benefit from our proposed concept.

This gives us insight to opportunities to explore.

Why in XR?

Creating a user journey of a scenario and tasks to do gives an idea of pain-points and opportunities of how completing it in XR benefits users.

Hypothesis

XR can help my user do (Tasks) and (Goals) in this scenario.

Insight

A list of potential features are created to rank against how well they support the technology and user.

Decision Matrix

Each specific feature is placed on a spreadsheet.

We assign points based on how closely they align with our goals for the user and the technology.

Defining Features

Create a clear breakdown of the features and goals targeted.

Scoping Features

We assigned story points to each feature creating epics to tackle the prototype.

Design Process

use case to prototype

Wireframe - Gray-boxing

At different steps of the design process, my team used Blender, Shape XR, or Unity to iterate on designs.

Blender

  • Create storyboards.
  • Think through poses and camera placement.
  • Capture the best angle to communicate concepts.

Shape XR

  • Place UI or 3d assets in XR space with user avatar models.
  • Scale, orient, and position in your real environment and human scale.

Unity

  • Iterate on pre-existing assets.
  • Make quick changes in the prototype.

Prototype

Each of our projects had their own functional unity prototype. They were demoed to our supervisor at the end of the project cycle.

Presentation

As part of check-ins our team prepared weekly slide deck presentations to outline features and communicate across our org.

Postmortem

Our team presented each prototype and we learned a little more on how we worked together as a team. We adjusted and learned how to lean into each other’s strengthens for the next project.

Reflection

I realized that the sorts of projects we created were too ambitious for what we wanted to achieve. We should have removed some of the not as essential features to show bite-sized successes before tackling everything a user might need.

Where I left SRA

I left with 3 projects successful prototypes completed.

What I would do next

Since these were prototypes, I would have liked to continue working on building out more features, but it would need to get picked up by a development team to continue working on them. I would have continued to given new solutions to create.
